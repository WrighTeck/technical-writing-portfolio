# Requirement Analysis Workflow

Requirement analysis is the first phase of the QA lifecycle.

Its purpose is to ensure requirements are clear, complete, testable, and risk-assessed before test planning begins.

---

## 1. Objectives

- Validate requirement clarity and completeness
- Identify ambiguities or missing acceptance criteria
- Detect high-risk areas early
- Confirm testability
- Identify integration and platform impact
- Prepare for traceability mapping

---

## 2. Requirement Review Process

1. Review Business Requirements Document (BRD) or User Stories.
2. Confirm acceptance criteria are clearly defined.
3. Identify edge cases and negative scenarios.
4. Identify calculation or data validation logic.
5. Confirm role-based access implications.
6. Identify impacted platforms (Web, Mobile, OTT, Desktop, Enterprise).
7. Identify integration dependencies.
8. Raise clarification questions to stakeholders.

---

## 3. Validation Questions

During analysis, confirm:

- What is the expected outcome?
- What are boundary conditions?
- What happens on invalid input?
- Are calculations clearly defined?
- Are error messages specified?
- Does this affect multiple systems?
- Are there device or OS implications?
- Are reporting impacts expected?

---

## 4. Risk Identification

Flag high-risk requirements such as:

- Financial or calculation logic
- Authentication or authorization changes
- API modifications
- Integration dependencies
- Cross-platform impact
- Data table updates (Enterprise systems)

High-risk requirements receive priority during test planning.

---

## 5. Traceability Preparation

- Assign requirement IDs.
- Map requirement IDs to future test cases.
- Identify regression impact areas.
- Prepare traceability matrix.

Traceability ensures complete coverage and audit readiness.

---

## 6. Output of Requirement Analysis

Deliverables from this phase include:

- Clarification questions
- Identified risk areas
- Platform impact summary
- Traceability framework
- Initial regression impact assessment
